What I’m working on at the moment: Smart Home (06/05/2021)

I love smart home set ups and I’m currently working on my own. So far, I’ve made most of the lights in my bedroom ‘smart’. As well as being able to control individual lights, I also have shortcuts set up to control multiple lights at once, and shortcuts that control a mix of lights and other things. I control all of these on my phone either: via my iPhone ‘remote’* or via Siri.

I have a few more ideas for my smart home set up. For example, although I have a smart plug to turn my floor lamp on and off, I’m unable to remotely control the strength of the light, so I’d like to build an implement to control the dimmer switches. I’d also like to be able to open and close my blinds remotely and to perform some tasks automatically when triggered by motion sensors. I’m not sure how to go about all of these but I do have some ideas. I’ve got a Raspberry Pi and the Pythonista app on iPhone so I’ll definitely be using these in some way!

I’ve had some initial thoughts of important things to consider:

  • I’ll need to keep a record of which ‘direction’ the motor went in previously because I don’t want to damage my lamp or blinds.

  • I’ll need two motors for each dimmer switch, these will need to be small in order to not get in one other’s way.

  • I’ll need one motor for the blinds.
